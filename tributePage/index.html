Sir Alexander Fleming

Discoverer of Lysozyme and Penicillin G

Here's a timeline of Sir Fleming's accomplishments:

  • 1881 - Born in Ayrshire, Scotland
  • 1908 - Joined St Mary's as a lecturer after being awarded a gold medal in bacteriology
  • 1909/1914 - Practiced as a venereologist between 1909 and 1914. He became the first doctor to administer a drug against syphilis called arsphenamine (Salvarsan)
  • 1921 - Saw the discovery of the antiseptic enzyme lysozyme
  • 1928 - Became a professor of bacteriology at the University of London
  • 1928 - Accidental finding wherein one on his fungus contaminated staphylococci culture destroyed all the surrounding staphylococci culture while other staphylococci colonies somewhat away were normal
    • 1929 - He initially called it “mould juice” but finally named the substance it produced Penicillin
  • 1944 - knighted as Knight Bachelor by King George VI to become Sir Alexander Fleming
  • 1945 - Alexander Fleming, Howard Florey and Ernst Boris Chain collectively received the Nobel Prize in Medicine
  • 1955 - Dies from a heart attack at the age of 73

-- Alexander Fleming
